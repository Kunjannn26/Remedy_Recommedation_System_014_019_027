Instructions on How to Take Ayurvedic Remedies

  • Ashwagandha

    Ashwagandha is available in various forms, including powder, capsules, and tablets. The recommended dosage is typically 600-1200mg per day, taken with meals or as directed by a healthcare professional.

  • Triphala

    Triphala can be consumed as a powder or capsule. For the powder, mix ½ to 1 teaspoon with warm water or juice and drink before bedtime. For the capsule, the recommended dosage is typically 500-1000mg per day, taken with meals or as directed by a healthcare professional.

  • Turmeric

    Turmeric is available in various forms, including fresh root, powder, and capsules. The recommended dosage is typically 400-600mg of standardized curcumin extract, taken 2-3 times per day with meals or as directed by a healthcare professional.

  • Ginger

    Ginger can be consumed fresh, powdered, or as a tea. The recommended dosage is typically 1-2g of fresh ginger per day, or 250-500mg of ginger extract taken 2-3 times per day with meals or as directed by a healthcare professional.

  • Neem

    Neem can be consumed in various forms, including capsules, powder, and oil. The recommended dosage is typically 500-1000mg per day of neem extract, or ½ to 1 teaspoon of neem powder mixed with warm water or juice, taken with meals or as directed by a healthcare professional.

  • Brahmi

    Brahmi can be consumed as a powder, capsule, or tea. The recommended dosage is typically 300-400mg per day of standardized extract, taken with meals or as directed by a healthcare professional. Brahmi tea can be made by steeping 1 teaspoon of dried Brahmi leaves in a cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes.

  • Licorice

    Licorice can be consumed in various forms, including tea, capsules, and tablets. The recommended dosage is typically 1-5g of dried root or 250-500mg of standardized extract, taken 1-3 times per day with meals or as directed by a healthcare professional.

  • Holy Basil

    Holy Basil can be consumed as a tea, capsule, or extract. The recommended dosage is typically 300-600mg per day of standardized extract, taken with meals or as directed by a healthcare professional. Holy Basil tea can be made by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried Holy Basil leaves in a cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes.

  • Fenugreek

    Fenugreek can be consumed as a powder, capsule, or tea. The recommended dosage is typically 500-1000mg per day of standardized extract, taken with meals or as directed by a healthcare professional. Fenugreek tea can be made by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of fenugreek seeds in a cup of hot water for 5-10 minutes.

  • Amla

    Amla can be consumed in various forms, including fresh fruit, powder, and capsules. The recommended dosage is typically 1-3g per day of amla powder or 500-1000mg of amla extract, taken with meals or as directed by a healthcare professional. Amla can also be incorporated into meals or consumed as a juice.
